Whether you are a compliance student taking your first steps, a junior analyst trying to understand how screening actually works, or a professional who wants to test concepts against real data: FinCrimeRadar gives you a free, open, and honest tool to learn with.

The story behind FinCrimeRadar

FinCrimeRadar was built by Pratik Zanke, a Financial Crime and Compliance professional with eight years of hands-on experience building AML programmes from scratch across multiple international markets.

After spending years working as a Senior Manager and Acting MLRO, holding regulatory accountability across four jurisdictions simultaneously. Pratik noticed a persistent gap: students and junior professionals entering the compliance field had no practical way to understand how screening actually works. The concepts exist in textbooks, but the hands-on experience of running a real screening check against live sanctions data was locked away inside enterprise systems that cost thousands per year.

FinCrimeRadar was built to fix that. With no prior coding experience, Pratik taught himself to build the platform from scratch, a fitting parallel to building AML frameworks from scratch across his career.

The screening tool

A live, free sanctions screening, PEP screening, and adverse media monitoring tool powered by OpenSanctions data (covering OFAC, UN, EU, OFSI and 40+ lists), a curated PEP database, and real-time news sources including BBC and OCCRP. Designed to show students and professionals exactly what a real screening check looks like.

The AML Knowledge Hub

Free, in-depth guides on UK financial crime law (MLR 2017, POCA 2002, Terrorism Act 2000), AML compliance obligations, and how screening works in practice. All content is verified against current FCA guidance and updated regularly.

Open source

FinCrimeRadar is open source. The code, data integrations, and screening engine are available on GitHub for anyone to inspect, use, or contribute to.

Important disclaimer

Educational tool only. FinCrimeRadar is designed as a free educational resource for compliance students and professionals. It is not a regulated compliance screening solution and must not be used as a substitute for commercial-grade AML screening in regulated firms. Results may not be complete, current, or accurate enough for compliance decision-making. Always verify against official sanction list sources and use FCA-approved or equivalent solutions for actual compliance obligations.

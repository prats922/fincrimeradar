What Scenario Lab trains you to do

Scenario Lab is a free, interactive KYC and KYB investigation trainer built for AML and financial crime compliance professionals. Each case presents a corporate ownership structure you have not seen before, and your task is to build out the ultimate beneficial ownership tree, identify each entity in that structure, screen the individuals against sanctions and PEP data, and reach a disposition decision, exactly as you would in a live case review.

The current release covers five cases spanning KYC and KYB scenarios of varying complexity, including nominee director structures, layered ownership through intermediate holding entities, and family relationships that require escalation judgement rather than a simple pass or fail. Every case is authored specifically for this tool, not adapted from a public enforcement action, so the scenarios stay original while reflecting the structural patterns compliance professionals encounter in real investigations.

How the investigation tools work

The ownership tree is built as an interactive node graph. Nodes stay unidentified until you investigate them, deliberately, because premature visual cues would defeat the purpose of practising judgement under incomplete information. A fuzzy matching threshold slider lets you control how tightly the screening engine matches names against the sanctions and PEP datasets, the same tradeoff between missed hits and false positives that governs real screening decisions. An optional PEP hint mode and a live risk calculator update as you work through the structure, giving you a running signal of how your investigation choices are shifting the overall risk picture.

Critically, a clear sanctions result on a direct entity is not treated as the end of the case. Where family relationships or nominee arrangements are present, the correct path requires escalation for a beneficial ownership determination, not automatic clearance, and the scoring in this tool reflects that. Scenario accuracy here is treated as a compliance obligation, not a content exercise, every case has been checked against how a real investigation would actually be expected to proceed.

This is a training environment, not a certification, and it carries no affiliation with ACAMS, ICA, or any credentialing body. It exists because the best practical training for AML investigation judgement is usually locked behind expensive enterprise platforms, and FinCrimeRadar's position is that practitioners should be able to build this muscle for free.
